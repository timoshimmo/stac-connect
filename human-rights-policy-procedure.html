Human Rights Policy

1.0 Policy Statement

STAC MARINE OFFSHORE is committed to respecting human rights in all aspects of our operations, including our FPSO vessel and interactions with stakeholders, employees, affected communities, and end-users. We recognize the importance of upholding human rights to maintain trust, confidence, and a positive reputation.

2.0 Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

  • 2.1 Outline our commitment to respecting human rights.
  • 2.2 Provide a framework for implementing and monitoring human rights responsibilities.
  • 2.3 Ensure that all stakeholders are aware of their rights and responsibilities.

3.0 Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, suppliers, and stakeholders of STAC MARINE OFFSHORE.

4.0 Key Principles

We are committed to upholding the following human rights principles:

  • 4.1 Respect for human rights
  • 4.2 Non-discrimination
  • 4.3 Fair labor practices
  • 4.4 Freedom of association
  • 4.5 Protection of vulnerable groups
  • 4.6 Community engagement
  • 4.7 Grievance Mechanism

5.0 Procedure

  • 5.1 Human Rights Due Diligence: Conduct regular human rights impact assessments to identify potential risks and opportunities.
  • 5.2 Training and Awareness: Provide regular training and awareness programs for employees on human rights.
  • 5.3 Grievance Mechanism: Establish a grievance mechanism to address human rights concerns and provide remedies.
  • 5.4 Monitoring and Reporting: Monitor human rights performance and report on progress.
  • 5.5 Stakeholder Engagement: Engage with stakeholders, including affected communities, to understand their concerns and develop mutually beneficial solutions.

6.0 Roles and Responsibilities

  • 6.1 Management: Oversee implementation of human rights policy and procedures.
  • 6.2 Human Resources: Provide training and awareness programs for employees.
  • 6.3 Operations: Implement human rights procedures in daily operations.
  • 6.4 Community Relations: Engage with affected communities and develop mutually beneficial.

7.0 Review and Revision

This policy will be reviewed and revised as necessary to ensure that it remains effective and aligned with international human rights standards and best practice.
